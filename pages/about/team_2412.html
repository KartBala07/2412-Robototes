About · Team 2412

The Robototes

The FIRST Robotics Competition team of Sammamish High School in Bellevue, Washington.

The Robototes, FRC Team 2412, was founded in 2007 at Sammamish High School with just 12 students and 2 mentors, competing for the first time in 2008. Our workspace changed frequently in the early years until 2015, when the team settled into Sammamish High School’s engineering and robotics rooms. Since then, enrollment has tripled — the team now consists of roughly 65 students and 20 mentors.

We compete in the Pacific Northwest district of the FIRST Robotics Competition. Our most successful seasons were 2014, 2019, and 2023, in which we won a total of 83 matches (not counting offseason events) and earned many awards. In total the team has competed across 18 seasons and 47 events, won 27 awards, and made 5 trips to the FIRST Championship.

When & where we meet

The team meets at Sammamish High School in the engineering room after school on Mondays, and four days a week during build season.

What we’re about

Build

Engineering, for real

Every year we get roughly six weeks to design, prototype, fabricate, wire, and program a full competition robot from scratch.

Compete

Pacific Northwest District

We compete at district events across Washington and Oregon, with the goal of qualifying for the District Championship and the FIRST Championship.

Inspire

Community first

From free tutoring to founding the Robotics STEM Advocacy Alliance, we work to make STEM accessible for everyone in our community.
